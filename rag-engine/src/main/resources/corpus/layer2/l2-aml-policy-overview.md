# Atlas Bank — Anti-Money-Laundering (AML) Program Policy

## Purpose
This policy establishes Atlas Bank's program to detect, prevent, and report money
laundering and terrorist financing in accordance with the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)
and applicable regulations. It applies to all employees, contractors, and lines
of business.

## Key reporting thresholds
- **Currency Transaction Report (CTR):** filed for cash transactions exceeding
  **$10,000** in aggregate by or on behalf of one person in a single business day.
- **Suspicious Activity Report (SAR):** filed when the bank knows, suspects, or has
  reason to suspect that a transaction (or pattern) aggregating **$5,000 or more**
  involves funds from illegal activity, is designed to evade BSA requirements
  (including **structuring** to avoid the CTR threshold), or has no apparent lawful
  purpose. SARs must be filed within **30 calendar days** of initial detection.

## Roles
- **Analysts** monitor alerts and document dispositions.
- **Compliance officers** review exceptions, approve SAR filings, and own the case
  record.
- The **BSA Officer** has final authority over the program.

## Confidentiality
The existence or contents of a SAR, and any supporting investigative material,
are strictly confidential and may not be disclosed outside authorized personnel.
